# Outreach Execution Skill

## Purpose
Plan and manage the logistics of getting pitches to journalists effectively — including exclusivity decisions, embargo management, timing strategy, sequencing, and follow-up cadence.

---

## Pre-Outreach Decisions

### Exclusivity vs. Embargo vs. Broad Release

Before any outreach begins, decide the distribution strategy:

**Exclusive**
- One journalist/outlet gets the story first, with a window of exclusivity (typically 24-72 hours before broad release)
- Best for: High-value announcements likely to generate feature coverage, stories where one outlet reaching the right audience is worth more than broad pickup
- Risk: If the exclusive journalist passes, you've lost time and may damage the relationship by pivoting
- Rule: Only offer exclusives to Tier A journalists where the relationship and fit justify it

**Tiered Embargo**
- Select journalists receive the story under embargo (agreement not to publish until a specified date/time) ahead of the public release
- Best for: Complex stories that benefit from journalist preparation time, announcements tied to a specific event or date
- Rule: Embargo dates must be realistic and respected. Do not break your own embargo. Put the embargo clearly in the subject line and first line of the pitch.

**Simultaneous Broad Release**
- All journalists receive the pitch at the same time, no exclusivity
- Best for: Hard news with immediate time sensitivity, announcements where broad pickup matters more than depth of any single story
- Can be combined with wire distribution

### Timing Strategy

**Day of week:**
- Tuesday, Wednesday, Thursday are generally best for pitching
- Monday: journalists are clearing weekend backlog
- Friday: stories get buried over the weekend
- Exception: breaking news or time-sensitive announcements override timing preferences

**Time of day:**
- 8:00-9:30 AM in the journalist's timezone is peak open time for pitch emails
- Avoid sending after 4 PM or before 7 AM
- For national outlets with East Coast journalists, Eastern Time drives the clock

**Lead time by outlet type:**

| Outlet Type | Recommended Lead Time |
|-------------|----------------------|
| National daily (digital) | 24-48 hours |
| National print magazine | 4-8 weeks |
| Trade press | 1-2 weeks |
| Broadcast (TV/radio) | 48-72 hours |
| Podcast | 2-4 weeks |
| Newsletter (weekly) | 5-7 days before issue |
| Wire services | Day-of or day-before |

## Sequencing Plan

Map outreach in order:

1. **Exclusive outreach** (if applicable): Day -3 to Day -1 before release date
2. **Embargo outreach to Tier A**: Day -2 to Day -1
3. **Embargo outreach to Tier B** (if applicable): Day -1
4. **Broad release / wire distribution**: Day 0, target 8-9 AM
5. **Tier C template outreach**: Day 0-1

Document the sequencing plan in writing before any pitches go out. This avoids accidental embargo breaks and ensures Tier A journalists are treated as priority.

## Follow-Up Cadence

Follow-up is expected but must be disciplined:

**First follow-up:**
- Timing: 2-3 business days after initial pitch
- Tone: Brief, helpful, low pressure
- Content: Add new information if possible (new data point, updated timing, additional access offered). Do not just say "just checking in."
- Length: 3-5 sentences maximum

**Second follow-up:**
- Timing: 4-5 business days after first follow-up
- Tone: Final check-in, make it easy for them to say no
- Content: "Happy to close the loop if this doesn't fit your coverage plans right now."
- Length: 2-3 sentences

**After two follow-ups with no response:**
- Close the loop internally and move on. Do not send a third follow-up.
- Note the non-response for future relationship management reference.

**Never:**
- Call journalists to follow up on email pitches unless you have an established relationship and they've asked for calls
- Send follow-ups over the weekend
- Express frustration or urgency in a follow-up

## Embargo Management Checklist

- [ ] Embargo date and time clearly stated in subject line (e.g., "EMBARGOED UNTIL [Date, Time, Timezone]")
- [ ] Embargo terms stated in first paragraph of pitch
- [ ] List of all journalists who received the embargoed release is documented
- [ ] Confirmation protocol defined (do we need journalist to acknowledge embargo, or proceed on assumption?)
- [ ] Plan for embargo break is defined (what do we do if someone publishes early?)
- [ ] Release date/time confirmed with internal stakeholders before any embargoes go out

---

## Asset Readiness Checklist

Before first pitch goes out, confirm these assets are ready:

- [ ] Final approved press release (or confirmed embargo version)
- [ ] High-resolution imagery (if relevant)
- [ ] Spokesperson bio and headshot
- [ ] Data set or supporting research (if announcement is data-driven)
- [ ] B-roll or video (for broadcast pitches)
- [ ] Spokesperson availability calendar (for the 48-72 hours around release)
- [ ] Approved Q&A / anticipated tough questions and answers

---

## Output Format
Produce an outreach execution plan including:
1. Distribution strategy recommendation (exclusive / embargo / broad) with rationale
2. Timing recommendation with rationale
3. Full sequencing timeline (day-by-day)
4. Follow-up schedule per journalist tier
5. Embargo management checklist (if applicable)
6. Asset readiness status
