# Journalist Intelligence Skill

## Purpose
Identify, research, categorize, score, and prioritize journalists and media outlets for a specific announcement. Replace instinct-only media list building with a structured, defensible targeting process.

---

## Contact Type Taxonomy

Before building any list, define which contact types are appropriate for this announcement. Different announcements warrant different mixes.

| Contact Type | Best For | Scoring Basis |
|---|---|---|
| **Beat journalist** | Hard news, breaking announcements, trade coverage | Beat alignment, outlet reach, recent coverage |
| **Columnist / commentator** | Opinion validation, trend analysis, thought leadership | Editorial fit, influence, audience trust |
| **Broadcast reporter** | Visual stories, consumer impact, major milestones | Visual angle strength, spokesperson availability |
| **Podcast host** | In-depth storytelling, niche audiences, spokesperson access | Topic fit, listener demographics, engagement |
| **Newsletter author** | Targeted B2B or niche audiences, high open rates | Subscriber fit, topic alignment, frequency |
| **Social media influencer** | Consumer products, lifestyle, community engagement | Engagement rate, audience demographics, topic authenticity |
| **Industry analyst** | B2B, enterprise, purchasing decisions, credibility | Firm reputation, coverage of this space, client base |
| **Blogger / independent expert** | Niche domains, technical depth, SEO amplification | Domain authority, topic expertise, audience loyalty |
| **Community moderator** | Grassroots reach, Reddit/Discord/Facebook communities | Community size, topic relevance, engagement quality |
| **Academic / researcher** | Policy, science, credibility validation | Field relevance, media presence, institutional authority |

## Journalist Identification Process

### Step 1: Define Target Coverage Types
Before searching for journalists, clarify what kinds of coverage we're seeking:
- **Breaking news coverage** — reporter files within 24-48 hours of release
- **Feature story** — longer piece where our announcement is a primary source
- **Trend piece** — our announcement as one data point in a broader industry story
- **Column/opinion** — influential commentator validates or analyzes our news
- **Podcast/audio** — spokesperson interview opportunity
- **Newsletter** — niche publication with highly targeted audience
- **Social amplification** — influencer/creator with strong engagement, not just reach
- **Community discussion** — moderator or admin who can seed conversation in relevant communities

Different coverage types require different contacts. Clarify which types are prioritized before building the list.

### Step 2: Define Target Audience Profile
Who is the ideal reader/viewer we want to reach through this coverage?
- Demographics (age, profession, geography, income)
- Knowledge level (general public vs. industry insider vs. policy audience)
- Geographic scope (local, regional, national, international)
- Platform preference (reads long-form / watches video / listens to podcasts / follows social)

This profile should drive contact selection, not just topic relevance.

### Step 3: Build the Target Contact List

For each contact, research and record:

| Field | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| Name | Full name |
| Outlet / Platform | Publication, show, channel, or community name |
| Contact type | Journalist / Influencer / Podcaster / Newsletter / Analyst / Blogger / Community |
| Beat / Topic focus | Specific coverage area or content niche |
| Platform type | Print / Digital / Broadcast / Podcast / Newsletter / Social / Community |
| Audience | Brief description of their readership/following |
| Audience size | Circulation, monthly uniques, followers, subscribers (approximate) |
| Engagement signal | For influencers/social: engagement rate estimate (high/medium/low) |
| Recent relevant content | 2-3 recent pieces/posts that show topic fit |
| Relationship status | Warm / Cool / Cold |
| Contact preference | Email / DM / LinkedIn / Booking form |
| Contact information | Email or preferred contact method if available |
| Relevance score | 1-5 (see scoring below) |
| Tier | A / B / C |

### Step 4: Relevance Scoring (1-5) — All Contact Types

**For journalists:**
- **5**: Covers this exact beat, recent relevant coverage, audience is our target audience
- **4**: Adjacent beat, demonstrated interest, high audience overlap
- **3**: Broader category, partial audience overlap
- **2**: Stretch connection, low audience overlap
- **1**: Volume-driven inclusion; unlikely to cover

**For influencers and social creators:**
- **5**: Posts regularly about this exact topic, audience demographics match perfectly, high engagement rate (5%+)
- **4**: Adjacent topic coverage, strong audience overlap, good engagement (2-5%)
- **3**: Broader lifestyle/industry fit, some audience overlap, moderate engagement
- **2**: Tangential content, limited audience overlap
- **1**: Reach only, no topic or audience fit

**For podcasters:**
- **5**: Show covers this exact topic, has featured similar announcements, interviews available
- **4**: Adjacent topic, demonstrated interest, audience fits
- **3**: Broader industry show, would fit as a segment
- **2**: General business/news show; our story is a stretch
- **1**: Wrong audience or topic for this show

**For analysts:**
- **5**: Covers this exact market segment, actively publishing on this topic
- **4**: Adjacent coverage, credible voice in this space
- **3**: Broader industry coverage, occasional relevant commentary
- **2**: Tangential; this falls outside their primary coverage
- **1**: Wrong firm or focus area

Only pitch contacts scoring 3 or above. Anything below 3 is noise regardless of follower count.

### Step 5: Tiering — All Contact Types

- **Tier A** — Top priority. Score 4-5. First outreach, potential exclusives, personalized approach. Target 5-15 contacts.
- **Tier B** — Strong secondary. Score 3-4. Outreach begins after Tier A. Target 10-25 contacts.
- **Tier C** — Broad distribution. Score 3. Template outreach acceptable. Volume is acceptable here.

## Platform Categorization

| Platform | Notes |
|----------|-------|
| National print/digital | Longest lead times, highest prestige, most competitive |
| Trade/industry press | High relevance to specialized audiences, faster turnaround |
| Local/regional | Strong for geographically specific announcements |
| Broadcast TV/radio | Requires visual angle or strong spokesperson availability |
| Podcast | Requires longer spokesperson time, more conversational format |
| Newsletter | Often underutilized, highly targeted, loyal audiences, high open rates |
| Social / influencer | Validate engagement rate, not just follower count; micro-influencers often outperform mega-influencers for niche topics |
| Community platforms | Reddit, Discord, Facebook Groups — seed discussion, not direct pitch; approach moderators first |
| Industry analysts | Longer lead time, briefing format preferred over press releases |

## Influencer-Specific Guidance

When including social media influencers:
- **Prioritize engagement rate over follower count.** A creator with 50K followers and 8% engagement reaches more engaged people than one with 500K and 0.5% engagement.
- **Verify topic authenticity.** Does this creator regularly post about this topic, or would it be an obvious paid/sponsored placement?
- **Check audience demographics.** Use available analytics signals (comments, audience language, referenced locations) to verify the audience matches the target.
- **Note platform.** Instagram influencer vs. LinkedIn creator vs. TikTok creator reach very different audiences even at similar follower counts.
- **Approach matters.** Influencers are pitched differently than journalists — more conversational, often requiring partnership discussion rather than a simple pitch.

---

## Relationship Intelligence

For each Tier A contact, note:
- Has this person covered us or our organization before?
- Have we provided them useful sources, data, or access in the past?
- Is there a warm introduction available through our network?
- Any known preferences or pet peeves?
- For influencers: have they mentioned our organization or competitors organically?

---

## Output Format
Produce a structured contact list with:
1. Executive summary of targeting approach and rationale
2. Contact type mix recommended for this announcement (e.g., 60% journalists, 25% influencers, 15% analysts)
3. Target audience profile
4. Full contact table organized by tier
5. Tier A deep-dives (one paragraph per contact on why they're top priority and how to approach)
6. Notes on any gaps (beats, platforms, or contact types where strong fits couldn't be found)
